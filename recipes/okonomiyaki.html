Okonomiyaki お好み焼き

Description:

A savory Japanese pancake made with a whole wheat flour batter and other ingredients. Often made with shredded cabbage, meat and seafood, topped with Okomomiyaki sauce, Japanese Mayo, Katusoboshi (Shaved Bonito flakes), Aonori and pickled ginger.

Region: Hiroshima, Osaka

Origin: Japan

Recipe Credit: Just One Cookbook



Serves 4

Prep: 30 minutes

Cook: 30 minutes

Resting Time: 1 hour

Total: 2 hours

Ingredients:

    Okonomiyaki Batter
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • 5.6oz mountain yam (can use grated daikon as well)
  • 3/4 cups Dashi stock (see here on how to make Dashi stock)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup tempura scraps
  • 1/4 cup pickled red ginger

    • Other Ingredients
  • 1 large cabbage
  • 1/2 Lb thinly sliced pork belly (can use bacon as well)
  • neautral flavored cooking oil

    • Okonomiyaki Sauce
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp sugar
  • 2 Tbsp oyster sauce
  • 4 Tbsp ketchup
  • 3 1/2 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce

    • Toppings
  • okonomi sauce
  • Kewpie mayonaise (Japanese Mayonaise)
  • katsuoboshi (dried bonito flakes)
  • aonori (dried green seaweed)
  • green onion
  • pickled red ginger

Method:

    Gather & prep all your ingredients.
  1. Discard the core of the cabbage and mince the leaves.
  2. Cut the pork belly slices in half and set aside.
  3. Peel and grate mountain yam (daikon) in a small bowl.

    4. Prepare Batter
  4. In a large bowl, combine 1 cup (120 g) all-purpose flour, ¼ tsp salt, ¼ tsp sugar, and ¼ tsp baking powder and mix all together.
  5. Add the grated nagaimo and dashi in the bowl.
  6. Mix all together till combined. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least one hour. Tip: This relaxes the gluten in the batter and improves the flavor and texture (fluffier). Some okonomiyaki shops refrigerate the batter overnight. Meanwhile, you can prepare the okonomiyaki sauce and other ingredients.

    7. Make Okonomiyaki Sauce
  7. Combine 1 ½ Tbsp sugar, 2 Tbsp oyster sauce, 4 Tbsp ketchup, and 3 ½ Tbsp Worcestershire sauce in a small bowl. Mix all together until sugar is completely dissolved.

    8. Cook Okonomiyaki
  8. After one hour, take out the batter from the refrigerator. Add 4 large eggs, ½ cup (8 Tbsp) tempura scraps, and ¼ cup (4 Tbsp) pickled red ginger in the bowl. Mix well until well-combined.
  9. Add chopped cabbage to the batter ⅓ at a time. Mix well before adding the rest.
  10. In a large pan, heat vegetable oil on medium heat. When the frying pan is hot (400ºF or 200ºC), spread the batter in a circle on the pan. If you’re new to making okonomiyaki, make a smaller and thinner size so it’s easier to flip.
  11. Place 2-3 sliced pork belly on top of okonomiyaki and cook covered for 5 minutes.
  12. When the bottom side is nicely browned, flip over.
  13. Gently press the okonomiyaki to fix the shape and keep it together. Cover and cook for another 5 minutes.
  14. Flip over one last time and cook uncovered for 2 minutes. If you’re going to cook the next batch, transfer to a plate.

    15. To Serve
  15. Apply okonomiyaki sauce with a brush or spoon, add Japanese mayonnaise in zigzagging lines (optional), and sprinkle dried bonito flakes (katsuobushi). You can also put dried green seaweed (aonori), chopped green onions, and pickled red ginger on top for garnish.
