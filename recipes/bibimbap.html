Bibimbap 비빔밥

Description:

One of the most famous Korean rice dishes. Topped with marinated meat, seasoned sautéed vegetables, kimchi, gochujang and often a fried egg.

Region: East Asia

Origin: Korea

Recipe Credit: The Woks of Life



Serves 4

Prep: 15 minutes

Cook: 30 minutes

Total: 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups medium grain white rice
  • 1 1/2 cups mung bean sprouts
  • 1 bunch watercress or spinach
  • 1 large carrot
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Salt
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil (divided)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (dark soy sauce, if you have it)
  • 4 eggs
  • ½ cup napa cabbage kimchi
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 2 teaspoons toasted sesame seeds

Method:

  1. Wash and prepare you rice in a rice cooker. If you don't have a rice cooker please use this information on how to prepare rice. Then put a medium pot of water on the stove, and bring to a boil.
  2. While the rice is cooking and water is heating up wash and prepare your vegetables. Julienne the carrot and mince the garlic.
  3. Once the water has come to a boil, blanch the beansprouts for 5 minutes. Remove from the water with a slotted spoon and drain. Then using the same water add the watercress and blanch for 1 minute. Set aside on a papertowel and wring out excess water.
  4. Rinse both the sprouts and the watercress under cold water to stop the cooking process. Drain again and wring out excess water from watercress. Roll the watercress into a log and slice 3-4 times then season with salt and sesame oil and set aside in a bowl. In a seperate bowl season the bean sprouts with salt and sesame oil. Divide the minced garlic between both bowls and lightly toss to comibine.
  5. In a skillet over medium-high heat, add a tablespoon of cooking oil and the julienned carrots and season lightly with salt. Cook stiring frequently till soft yet lightly crunchy, around 1 minute. Remove from pan and set aside.
  6. Return the pan to the stove and over medium-high heat add the ground beef and cook till browned, making sure to break up into smaller pieces. Stir in soy sauce. Once done remove, set aside and wipe pan. Add cooking oil over medium heat and fry each egg individualy. Sunny side up is the best.
  7. Divide the rice amongst 4 bowls and top with seasoned beef, watercress, carrots, bean sprouts, kimchi and the fried egg. Serve with a dollop of gochujang and sesame seeds.
